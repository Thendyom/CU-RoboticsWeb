Bachelor's thesis guide

Build the thesis around a question that can be answered

A strong RIS thesis connects one clear research question to a reproducible investigation. The written structure should make that connection visible from the introduction through the conclusion.

01

Question and scope

Define the practical problem, operating conditions, success criteria, and the work that remains outside the thesis. State the research questions before describing the implementation.

02

Investigation

Establish a baseline, document fixed conditions, compare controlled design choices, and preserve the configurations, calibration values, code, and logs needed to reproduce the work.

03

Evaluation

Select metrics that match the intended use. Report repeated trials, relevant operating conditions, resource costs, and failure cases rather than only the best result.

04

Discussion

Separate measured results from inferred explanations. Explain what the evidence supports, where it does not generalize, and what each limitation means for the conclusion.

05

Conclusion and record

Answer every research question directly. Keep detailed configurations, additional trials, and supporting material in the appendix so the main argument stays readable and the work remains auditable.

Before submission

  • Every headline claim points to measured evidence or a cited source.
  • Requirements, assumptions, interfaces, and test conditions are explicit.
  • Main results and exploratory work are clearly separated.
  • The conclusion answers each research question within the limits of the evidence.
  • The final PDF, figures, tables, citations, and appendices are checked page by page.

Academic Work

What good student work looks like

Good projects are narrow enough to finish, documented enough to reproduce, and honest about limitations. For robotics, that usually means clear experimental conditions, checked data, working code, and a written argument that explains what changed and why it matters.

Students should also expect a lot of ordinary engineering work: debugging, documenting assumptions, reading papers slowly, and being precise about what the robot or model did not do.
