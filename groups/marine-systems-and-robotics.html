Research Group

Marine Systems & Robotics

Led by Prof. Dr. Francesco Maurelli, Marine Systems and Robotics develops intelligent capabilities for autonomous platforms.

Group Leader

Prof. Dr. Francesco Maurelli

Constructor University lists Maurelli as Associate Professor of Marine Systems with a focus on Marine Robotics. His group applies robotics, automation, AI, and algorithmic methods to autonomous robots in the marine domain and beyond.

Faculty profile Official group page

Research themes

  • Positioning and navigation methods
  • Filtering and data fusion
  • Fault management
  • Perception with inertial, acoustic, video, and LiDAR sensing
  • Autonomous real-time decision-making

Research focus areas

The group connects marine robotics with concrete machine-intelligence methods: localisation, sonar perception, data fusion, autonomy architecture, and field evaluation.

AUV localisation

Navigation under water is hard because many ordinary positioning assumptions break. This is why filtering, active localisation, and uncertainty matter.

Perception

Marine perception often has to work with acoustic, visual, inertial, and LiDAR signals under incomplete or difficult conditions.

Autonomy

Autonomous systems need to recognize failures, manage uncertainty, and keep operating when communication or sensing is limited.
