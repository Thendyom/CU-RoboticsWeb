Research themes in difficult environments

Constructor Robotics lists work across marine robotics, autonomous driving, response robots, space robotics, logistics, and robotics education. RIS students meet these themes through coursework, labs, projects, internships, and thesis work.

Research Domains

The public Constructor Robotics research page groups the work around autonomous systems in unstructured domains. This page summarizes the areas most relevant for a first RIS research orientation.

Marine

Marine Robotics

UUV, AUV, ROV, and ASV systems for underwater and surface operations, mapping, inspection, and localisation.

Mobility

Autonomous Driving

ADAS, scene understanding, human-centered AI, and perception for transportation and urban mobility.

Field Robotics

Response and Space Robots

Robots for safety, security, rescue, planetary exploration, caves, and other spaces where autonomy must be robust.

Industry

Automation in Logistics

Autonomous handling of goods, mobile manipulation, and production-facing robotics.

Teaching

Robotics Education

Hands-on lab work, online robotics infrastructure, project-based learning, and research-oriented teaching.

Perception

3D Mapping and Sonar

Environment models, sonar data, SLAM, and perception pipelines for poor-visibility conditions.
