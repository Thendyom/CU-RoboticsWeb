Maurelli and Marine Systems

Constructor University lists Prof. Dr. Francesco Maurelli as Associate Professor of Marine Systems with a focus on Marine Robotics. The Marine Systems and Robotics group page lists him as group leader.

Associate Professor of Marine Systems with a focus on Marine Robotics

Maurelli's group applies robotics, automation, artificial intelligence, and algorithmic methods to autonomous robots in the marine domain: underwater, on the surface, and above the water.

Research Group

Marine Systems and Robotics

The group develops intelligent capabilities for autonomous platforms, with emphasis on positioning and navigation, advanced filtering and data fusion, fault management, and perception.

The official group description names inertial, acoustic, video, and LiDAR sensing as part of its work on autonomous real-time decision-making. Its application range is wider than the marine domain alone, including research from underwater systems to space.

Research themes

  • Autonomous underwater vehicles and surface systems
  • Navigation, localisation, SLAM, and sonar perception
  • Filtering, data fusion, and fault management
  • Marine robotics education and field research
