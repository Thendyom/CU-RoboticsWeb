Program Chair

Prof. Dr. Francesco Maurelli

Associate Professor of Marine Systems with a focus on Marine Robotics

Maurelli leads the Marine Systems and Robotics group. His work addresses autonomous robots in the marine domain: underwater, on the surface, and above the water. The research extends beyond individual platforms to localisation, perception, data fusion, autonomy, and field operation.

Research Group

Marine Systems and Robotics

Marine Systems and Robotics provides a direct connection between RIS and autonomous-systems research. The group develops intelligent capabilities for autonomous platforms, with emphasis on positioning and navigation, filtering and data fusion, fault management, and perception.

Its work combines inertial, acoustic, video, and LiDAR sensing with autonomous real-time decision-making, bringing software, sensing, and systems work into the same research setting.

Research themes

  • AUV localisation and underwater navigation
  • Sonar, visual, inertial, and LiDAR perception
  • Filtering, data fusion, and fault management under uncertainty
  • Autonomy architectures that can survive limited communication and imperfect sensing

Constructor Robotics context

Constructor Robotics describes the research focus as autonomous systems, ranging from embedded hardware, mechatronics, and sensors to high-level software. It also places 3D perception, world modelling, and cooperation among the core themes of robotics research at the university.

Research environment
