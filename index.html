Program Snapshot

Autonomous systems, taught with engineering discipline

Robotics and Intelligent Systems is an English-taught Bachelor of Science program in the School of Computer Science & Engineering. The curriculum focuses on autonomous mobile systems, with applications in robotics, automation, transportation, communication technologies, marine technology, and logistics.

For Students

From foundations to research work

The first year builds the foundation in mathematics and physics, programming, algorithms, and electrical and computing systems. The second year moves into robotics, AI, automation, embedded systems, control systems, computer vision, machine learning, and the RIS project.

The third year adds specialization choices, the mandatory internship, a study-abroad window, and the extended Bachelor's thesis. Students interested in research should connect coursework, papers, and a focused technical question before approaching a supervisor.

Thesis preparation

Read recent group papers before asking for a topic. A useful first conversation starts with a method, an application area, and a realistic project boundary.

Bachelor's thesis guide

Research practice

Good student work is reproducible: clear code, checked data, short progress notes, and written arguments that can survive review.

Lab work

Robotics work combines software, hardware, experiments, and failure analysis. The path is technical, but it is learnable with consistent preparation.

Research Focus

Marine systems, field autonomy, and difficult sensing

RIS connects naturally to the Marine Systems and Robotics group led by Prof. Dr. Francesco Maurelli. The group works on autonomous platforms, positioning and navigation, filtering and data fusion, fault management, and perception with inertial, acoustic, video, and LiDAR sensing.

Marine robotics

Autonomous underwater and surface systems, sonar, localisation, mapping, inspection, and field operations.

AUV localisation

Filtering, active localisation, SLAM, and navigation for robots that cannot rely on ordinary terrestrial positioning.

Field autonomy

Autonomous behavior in environments where sensing is incomplete, communication is limited, and failures need to be detected early.

Research Entry

Research questions start with method and evidence

Students usually enter research through a focused method rather than a broad domain. In marine robotics that might mean AUV localisation, sonar perception, uncertainty-aware navigation, data fusion, or fault handling in autonomous systems.
