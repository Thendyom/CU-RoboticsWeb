Program Snapshot

Autonomous systems, taught as engineering

Robotics and Intelligent Systems is a BSc program in the School of Computer Science and Engineering. The program covers the engineering methods behind autonomous mobile systems, with applications in robotics, transportation, communication technologies, marine technology, and logistics.

For Students

From fundamentals to research work

The first part of the program builds the mathematical, physical, programming, and electrical background needed for robotics. Later modules move into robotics, AI, automation, embedded systems, control, computer vision, machine learning, and a dedicated RIS project.

The final year gives room for specialization, internships or study abroad, and an extended Bachelor's thesis. Students who want research experience should approach faculty early with a concrete topic area, relevant coursework, and a realistic plan for independent work.

Thesis Preparation

Read recent group papers, identify the method you want to work on, and discuss a focused project scope before thesis registration.

Research Practice

Student work should be reproducible: clear code, checked data, short progress notes, and written arguments that can survive review.

Lab Work

Robotics work combines software, hardware, experiments, and failure analysis. The site should make that path visible to new students.

Research Connection

Connected to robotics groups

RIS teaching is tied to Constructor Robotics and the Marine Systems and Robotics group led by Prof. Dr. Francesco Maurelli. Research themes include autonomous platforms, positioning and navigation, filtering and data fusion, fault management, perception, underwater systems, space robotics, and robotics education.

Marine Robotics

Autonomous underwater and surface systems, sonar, localization, mapping, inspection, and field operations.

Autonomous Driving

AI, vision, human-centered computing, scene understanding, and mobility applications in urban contexts.

Space and Unstructured Domains

Robotic systems for planetary exploration, caves, response robots, logistics, and other difficult environments.
